Q1. Knowledge Representation (10 marks)
Create a Prolog knowledge base to represent the following information:

Courses and their prerequisites.

Professors teaching courses.

Students enrolled in courses.

Departments offering courses.

Example data to include:

Courses: cs101, cs201, cs301, math201, ai401.

Prerequisites: cs201 requires cs101, cs301 requires cs201, ai401 requires both cs301 and math201.

Professors: dr_smith teaches cs101, dr_lee teaches ai401.

Students: alice enrolled in cs101, bob in cs201, carol in ai401.

Departments: computer_science offers cs101, cs201, cs301, ai401; mathematics offers math201.

I included some extra facts here (if you want to enhance and test your logic )   download here https://eclass.yorku.ca/pluginfile.php/7591020/mod_assign/intro/Extra-facts.txt?time=1759081104223

Q2. Reasoning with Rules (10 marks)
Define rules in Prolog for the following:

can_take(Student, Course) – a student can take a course if they are enrolled in all its prerequisites.

teaches(Professor, Student) – a professor teaches a student if the professor teaches a course in which the student is enrolled.

same_department(Course1, Course2) – two courses are from the same department if they are offered by the same department.

Q3. Recursive Reasoning (10 marks)
Define a recursive rule:

prerequisite_chain(Course, PreCourse) – true if PreCourse is a direct or indirect prerequisite of Course.

Example: prerequisite_chain(ai401, cs101) should succeed.

Q4. Queries (10 marks)
Write Prolog queries (with expected results) for the following:

Which courses can Alice take?

Which students are taught by Dr. Lee?

Which courses are in the Computer Science department?

What are all indirect prerequisites of ai401?

Q5. Extension (10 marks)
Extend your knowledge base to represent course scheduling conflicts (e.g., cs201 and math201 occur at the same time).

Define a predicate conflict(Student, Course1, Course2) that is true if a student is enrolled in two courses scheduled at the same time.

Hint:  (sample data)


time(cs101, mon_9am).
time(cs201, tue_9am).
time(cs301, wed_9am).
time(math201, tue_9am).
time(ai401, thu_11am).
